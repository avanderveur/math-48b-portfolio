Vertical & Horizontal Asymptotes

Vertical asymptotes come from denominator zeros that do not cancel. Horizontal asymptotes describe the end behavior of the function.

Vertical Asymptotes (VA)

Vertical asymptotes occur at x-values that make the denominator zero after simplifying.

1

Factor the denominator completely.

2

Cancel any common factors with the numerator.

3

Set remaining denominator factors = 0 to find VA(s).

Horizontal Asymptotes (HA)

Horizontal asymptotes are determined by comparing polynomial degrees:

1

deg(top) < deg(bottom): HA is y = 0

2

deg(top) = deg(bottom): HA = ratio of leading coefficients

3

deg(top) > deg(bottom): no horizontal asymptote

Example 1 (Animated + Real Graph)

\( f(x) = \dfrac{2x + 1}{x - 3} \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Denominator: x − 3
Denominator = 0 → x = 3
Degrees equal → HA = 2/1 = 2

Example 2 (Animated + Real Graph)

\( g(x) = \dfrac{x^2 + 4}{2x^2 - 8x} \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Denominator: 2x(x − 4)
Set to zero → x = 0, 4
Degrees equal → HA = 1/2

Summary

Vertical asymptotes come from denominator zeros that do not cancel. Horizontal asymptotes come from comparing polynomial degrees.
