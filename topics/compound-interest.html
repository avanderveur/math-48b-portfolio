Compound Interest (Discrete)

Discrete compound interest models money growing by repeated multiplication at regular intervals.

Compound Interest Formula

An account with discrete compounding grows by:

\( A(t) = P\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{n}\right)^{nt} \)
P

Principal (starting amount)

r

Annual interest rate (decimal)

n

Compounds per year (monthly = 12, quarterly = 4)

t

Time in years

A(t)

Amount after t years

Repeated Multiplication Meaning

Every compounding period multiplies the money by a growth factor.

Growth factor = \( 1 + \dfrac{r}{n} \)

Then we apply that factor nt times.
