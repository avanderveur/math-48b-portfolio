Rational Inequalities + Sign Chart Method

Solve rational inequalities by finding critical points, building a sign chart, and choosing intervals that satisfy the inequality.

Sign Chart Method (Checklist)

1

Put everything on one side so you have 0 on the other.

2

Factor numerator and denominator completely.

3

Find critical points (zeros of numerator & denominator).

4

Place critical points on a number line.

5

Test signs on each interval → build sign chart.

6

Select intervals that satisfy the inequality. Use closed/open dots correctly.

Example 1 (Animated)

\( \dfrac{x+1}{x-2} > 0 \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Numerator = 0 → \( x = -1 \)
Denominator = 0 → \( x = 2 \)
Critical points → \( -1, 2 \)
(-∞, -1): positive
(-1, 2): negative
(2, ∞): positive

Example 2 (Animated)

\( \dfrac{(x-3)(x+2)}{x+1} \le 0 \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Zeros of numerator → \( x = -2, 3 \)
Denominator = 0 → \( x = -1 \)
Critical points → \( -2, -1, 3 \)
(-∞, -2): negative
(-2, -1): positive
(-1, 3): negative
(3, ∞): positive

Summary

Rational inequalities are solved by sign analysis on intervals. Zeros may be included depending on ≥ or ≤, while denominator zeros are never included.
