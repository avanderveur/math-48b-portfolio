Inverse Functions + Reflection Over y = x

An inverse function “undoes” the original function. Graphically, a function and its inverse are mirror images across the line \(y = x\).

Core Definition

\( f \text{ and } f^{-1} \text{ are inverses if } f(f^{-1}(x)) = x \text{ and } f^{-1}(f(x)) = x \)
1

Write \(y = f(x)\).

2

Swap \(x\) and \(y\).

3

Solve for \(y\).

4

Rename \(y\) as \(f^{-1}(x)\).

Graph Meaning

If \((a, b)\) lies on \(f(x)\), then \((b, a)\) lies on \(f^{-1}(x)\).

Reflection line: \(y = x\)

Example 1 (Animated + Reflection)

\( f(x) = 2x + 3 \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Start: \( y = 2x + 3 \)
Swap x/y: \( x = 2y + 3 \)
Solve for y: \( y = \dfrac{x - 3}{2} \)
Inverse: \( f^{-1}(x) = \dfrac{x - 3}{2} \)

Example 2 (Animated + Reflection)

\( g(x) = (x - 1)^3 + 2 \)
Press Next or Play to start.
Start: \( y = (x - 1)^3 + 2 \)
Swap x/y: \( x = (y - 1)^3 + 2 \)
Solve for y: \( y = \sqrt[3]{x - 2} + 1 \)
Inverse: \( g^{-1}(x) = \sqrt[3]{x - 2} + 1 \)

Summary

Inverses swap input/output. Algebraically we swap \(x\) and \(y\) then solve for \(y\). Graphically the two curves are reflections across \(y = x\).
