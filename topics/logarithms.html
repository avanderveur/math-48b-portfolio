Logarithmic Functions (Intro + Converting Forms)

Logarithms are the inverse of exponential functions. They answer the question: “What exponent gives this value?”

Core Definition

logb(x) = y ⇔ by = x
b

Base (must be > 0 and not equal to 1)

x

Argument (must be > 0)

y

Exponent you need on b to get x

Natural Log

The natural log uses base e ≈ 2.718…

ln(x) = loge(x)

Example 1 (Animated Conversion + Evaluate)

log3(81)
Press Next or Play to start.
Start: log₃(81) = y
Convert: 3y = 81
Recognize: 81 = 3⁴
So: y = 4

Example 2 (Convert Exponential → Log)

5x = 1/125
Press Next or Play to start.
Start: 5x = 1/125
Convert: log₅(1/125) = x
Rewrite: 1/125 = 5⁻³
So: x = −3

Log Graph Basics

A basic logarithm is y = logb(x). Its domain is x > 0 and it has a vertical asymptote at x = 0.

Summary

Logs convert between exponential and logarithmic forms. They solve for exponents and are inverses of exponentials. A log graph has domain x > 0 and a vertical asymptote at x = 0.
