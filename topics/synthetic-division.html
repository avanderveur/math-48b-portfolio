Synthetic Division

A shortcut for dividing polynomials when the divisor is linear, in the form \((x - c)\).

What Is Synthetic Division?

Synthetic division is a faster alternative to polynomial long division. We only use coefficients and repeat a cycle of bring down → multiply → add. The last value is the remainder.

You can use synthetic division when the divisor looks like:
$$x - c$$
(If it’s \((x + 4)\), then \(c = -4\).)

Steps for Synthetic Division

1

Write coefficients in descending powers. Use 0 for missing terms.

2

Identify \(c\) from the divisor \((x - c)\).

3

Bring down the first coefficient to the bottom row.

4

Multiply that number by \(c\) and write it in the middle row.

5

Add the column.

6

Repeat multiply + add until the final column.

How to Read the Final Row

The bottom row gives the quotient coefficients. The last number is the remainder.

If the final row reads:
$$a,\; b,\; c,\; r$$
then the quotient is:
$$ax^2 + bx + c$$
and the remainder is:
$$r$$
